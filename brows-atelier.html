Brows & lashes FAQs

Before your first visit.

How long does brow lamination last?

Most clients get 6–8 weeks of hold before the shape starts to soften. We pair lamination with a brow wax & tint every 3–4 weeks in between to keep the look polished.

What's the difference between a Korean lash lift and a regular lash lift?

Korean lash lift uses a more recent, gentler chemistry that lifts from the root and gives a more natural curve. Traditional lifts curl the lash itself; Korean lifts angle the lash upward for a softer, more "awake" look that suits more lash types.

Can I wear makeup after?

Skip eye makeup and water for the first 24 hours after a lamination or lift. After that, normal routine is fine — including mascara if you'd like (most clients skip it).

I have very thin or sparse brows. Is lamination right for me?

Lamination works best on clients who have some brow density to shape. If your brows are very sparse, we'll talk through tint-only options or a slower approach at your first visit.

How do you decide on a brow shape?

I map the brow to your bone structure and natural growth pattern, not a trending shape. We'll talk through what you've tried before, what you've liked and disliked, and build from there.

What if I'm new to brows & lashes entirely?

Welcome — about half of new clients start here. A first-time brow wax + tint or a classic lash lift is a great introduction. We'll figure out the cadence together.
